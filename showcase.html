Part 2 · "Live-Wire" status-motion

red / yellow / green expressed through the primitives above. Sandbox prototype — timings are first-draft.

North-star rule: Getting worse is sudden & loud. Getting better is smooth & quiet. Staying bad is a patient loop. Being fine is silent.
① Transition grammar — fire an event, feel the verb
CAT 305
Idle
★
Red jolts then settles into a patient loop. Green washes once and goes silent forever. Yellow creeps in. Milestone completions get the brand stamp. (On a phone each also fires a haptic twin — no-op on desktop.)
② Tempo as a clock — same yellow, the pulse counts down
Due soon 7 days out
Drag toward 0 — the breathe quickens as the deadline nears. Clamped to a floor so it never strobes. Under reduced-motion this collapses to the number, which is why the readout always shows.
③ Interaction layer — hover each (status owns the border)
Red zips the border (urgent), yellow breathes (patient), green sweep-fills and settles (go). Same three personalities as the transitions — one language.
Sandbox only. Not imported anywhere — index.html, app.js, style.css untouched. Open straight off disk. If the feel lands, we lock timings + resolve the open tensions (red-split, budget eviction, reduced-motion equivalents) in a spec, then stamp it into the rulebook via /jactec-ui.
