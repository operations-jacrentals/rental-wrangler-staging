Inspection — a live capture surface

Replaces the outdated "tap a button, fill a separate form" Inspection (spec §6, 2026-07-20). Framed as one section of a Unit's inline-expanded card — same section-chip rail as Yard Journey across the top, Inspection active. The checklist stays open live in the section: each item is captured in place, a progress head tracks "N of M captured" plus a rollup Signal (worst item wins), and the single Door stays a disabled/ghost lock until every required item is captured — it can only be pressed when the inspection is actually done. Gator (left) is interactive — capture the last two items and press the Door yourself. Scout (right) shows the calm afterimage once it's finished.

Inspection is now a section, not a button that launches a form — the checklist lives open in place, and answers fill in as you go. Notes for review:
  • The progress head carries "N of M captured" (a value you read → bold, body-voice, on the size ladder) + the rollup Signal (worst item wins) + the one Door. Nothing else on the section competes for a second action.
  • Four item states, same vocabulary used everywhere else — grey = not captured, green = pass, dimmed yellow (#eed44b) = due now, filled red = failed. No new colour language.
  • Hydraulics failing raises the whole section to red and auto-spawns a linked WO Ref — expand that row (it opens by default here) to see it; the fact stays visible even once the inspection is marked done.
  • The Door is gated, not just greyed — it carries a lock icon + tooltip naming exactly what's left, matching the same honest-lock pattern as the money-gated Door in the Units detail mock. Capture the last two items on Gator and it unlocks to solid commit-blue; press it and the whole head flips to a plain Completed Stamp — proving "done" is a real state change, not a lie.
  • Done ≠ clean. Marking the inspection done does not soften or hide the still-open Hydraulics flag — that's the whole point of "the inspection only flips to Done when it's actually done," not when someone taps past it.
  • Scout (right) is the calm afterimage: once truly finished, the Door disappears rather than staying an inert button — honest-affordance: not tappable ⇒ it doesn't look tappable.
  • Matte, no glow; dark-theme --txt3 nudged #717b89→#7f8896 (measured 4.2:1 → 5.0:1 on --card) to clear the AA text floor — everything else is the exact palette from Yard Journey / Detail views, reused for continuity.
